In function golf, the objective is simple.
Given a list of functions, and two numbers
$n$ and $m$, apply functions to $n$ until
you reach $m$. For example, if:
\begin{align*}
 A(x) &= x^2 \\
 B(x) &= x-1 \\
 n &= 10 \\
 m &= 36 && \text{golf for $m$ starting with $n$}
\end{align*}
then one solution is:
\begin{align*}
 m = n \rhd B \rhd B \rhd B \rhd B \rhd A
\end{align*}
